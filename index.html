Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 29, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak in 2035. The projected nominal GDP growth rate of 7.0% falls short of the required 18.0% annual growth needed to reach the rich country threshold of $13,000 nominal per capita by 2035. Additionally, structural constraints such as providing productive jobs for a rising working-age population and navigating geopolitical uncertainties pose further challenges.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India's GDP growth rate for 2023 was 7.58%, which is below the required sustained high growth rates of around 10% needed to rapidly close the income gap with developed economies.
  2. India needs to create about 90 million non-farm jobs by 2030 to absorb its growing workforce, but current job creation rates are significantly lower, with only about 3.5 million jobs created annually in recent years.
  3. India's labor productivity growth has been around 6.5% annually, which is lower than the 8-10% growth needed to match global benchmarks and drive economic transformation.
  4. India aims to invest about 10% of its GDP in infrastructure annually, but current investments are around 7-8%, leading to a shortfall in critical infrastructure development.
  5. India's literacy rate is about 77.7%, which is below the 90% threshold seen in many developed economies, indicating a need for significant improvement in human capital development.
