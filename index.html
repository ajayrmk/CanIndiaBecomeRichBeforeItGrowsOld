Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 28, 2025)

India is unlikely to become rich before it grows old because the required nominal annual growth rate of 18.0% to reach the rich country threshold of $13,000 nominal GNI per capita within 10 years far exceeds the projected actual nominal growth rate of about 7.0% per year. Similarly, the required PPP growth rate of 5.9% slightly surpasses the projected actual PPP growth rate of 5.0%, indicating a shortfall in growth momentum needed to achieve high-income status before the demographic peak in 2035.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. Manufacturing sector contribution* remains at ~16% of GDP (MoSPI 2023), below the 25% target required for middle-income transition (NITI Aayog's Make in India 2.0 blueprint).
  2. Labor productivity growth* stagnates at 2.38% annually (Statista 2025), half the 5% needed to offset aging workforce costs (World Bank's India Country Report 2023).
  3. Formal employment coverage* shows only 131.5 lakh EPFO enrollments annually (PIB 2024), leaving 85% of workers in informal sectors without social security (ILO-India Collaboration Reports 2023).
  4. Female labor participation* at 37.8% employment rate (Forbes India 2024) trails the 50% threshold required for demographic dividend utilization (UNDP India Policy Briefs 2023).
  5. Infrastructure investment* at 1.7% of GDP (RBI 2023) falls short of the 2.5-3% needed to support 7%+ GDP growth (IMF's India Infrastructure Gap Analysis 2023).
