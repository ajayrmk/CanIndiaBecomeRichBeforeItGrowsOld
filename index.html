Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 27, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak in 2035. The country needs an annual nominal growth rate of 18.0% and a PPP growth rate of 5.9% to reach the rich country threshold, but projected actual growth rates are lower, at around 7.0% nominal and 5.0% PPP. Additionally, India's current GNI per capita is $2,480.8 (nominal) and $7,340.0 (PPP), far below the rich country threshold of $13,000 per capita.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. Manufacturing sector contribution* at 27.6% of GDP (2023) versus the 32.3% benchmark (2011) required to sustain industrial job creation and export competitiveness, reflecting a 4.7 percentage point decline over 12 years.
  2. Youth unemployment rate* of 15% against the need for <5% to absorb 10-12 million annual labor market entrants, creating a 10 percentage point gap in employment generation capacity.
  3. Labor productivity growth* lagging behind capital-driven growth, with 90% informal workforce trapped in low-productivity sectors compared to <20% informal employment in advanced economies, limiting per-worker output gains.
  4. Female labor force participation* at 26% (2023) versus the 47% global average, representing a 21 percentage point deficit in human capital utilization.
  5. Required job creation* of 145-330 million by 2050 (IMF) against current annual formal job growth of ~1 million, necessitating a 10-20x acceleration in employment generation rates to meet demographic demands.
