Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of March 30, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak. The projected nominal growth rate of 7.0% falls short of the needed 18.0% annual increase to reach the rich country threshold by 2035. Additionally, structural constraints such as a large informal workforce, depressed consumer demand, and a widening trade deficit pose substantial barriers to rapid economic advancement.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India needs an annual GDP growth rate of at least 8% to become a developed nation by 2047, but it is projected to grow at 6.5% in FY26, falling short of the required threshold.
  2. India faces a significant jobs crisis with an unemployment rate of 4.2% and youth unemployment at 15%, while needing to create millions of jobs annually to absorb its large workforce.
  3. India's workforce productivity is among the lowest globally, with the labor force being the second-largest in the world but having low productivity levels.
  4. Only 46% of India's cultivable land is irrigated, indicating a significant gap in agricultural infrastructure development.
  5. India's education spending is currently at 4.6% of GDP, which needs to rise to 6.5% by FY2048 to support its young population and workforce requirements.
