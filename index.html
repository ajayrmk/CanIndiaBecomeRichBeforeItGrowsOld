Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 15, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak in 2035. The country needs an annual nominal growth rate of 18.0% and a PPP growth rate of 5.9% to reach the rich country threshold, but current projections indicate a nominal growth rate of about 7.0% and a PPP growth rate of 5.0%. Additionally, structural constraints such as food inflation, stagnant job growth, and trade deficits hinder India's economic progress.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India's GDP growth rate is projected at around 6.5% to 7.02% in recent years, which is high but may not be sufficient to bridge the gap with developed economies quickly enough. For instance, to achieve rapid economic transformation, some emerging economies have targeted growth rates closer to 10% annually.
  2. Despite a large workforce of 586 million, India faces jobless economic growth, with low workforce productivity levels. The unemployment rate remains relatively high, indicating a need for more effective job creation strategies.
  3. India's workforce productivity is among the lowest globally, contributing to inefficient economic output. For example, improving productivity by even a few percentage points could significantly enhance GDP growth.
  4. India's infrastructure spending is crucial for growth but often falls short. For instance, the government aims to increase infrastructure spending, but current allocations may not meet the required scale to support rapid economic expansion.
  5. India's social welfare spending, including education and healthcare, stands at about 8.6% of GDP, which is lower than the OECD average. Increasing this investment could improve human capital and support long-term economic growth.
