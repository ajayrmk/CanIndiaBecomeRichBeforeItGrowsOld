Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 14, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak. The country's projected GDP growth rates, ranging from 6.4% to 6.8% for the fiscal year 2024-2025, are below the ambitious targets needed to elevate GNI per capita to rich country thresholds. Furthermore, structural issues such as stagnant job growth, persistent inflation, and a rising trade deficit hinder India's ability to sustain high growth rates necessary for rapid wealth creation. Demographically, India has about 10 years before its population peaks, but achieving the required annual growth rates of 18% in nominal terms or 5.9% in PPP terms is unlikely with current economic policies and projections.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India's GDP growth rate is projected to be between 6.5% and 7.3% in the coming years, which is below the 8% to 10% required for rapid economic transformation to high-income status before aging.
  2. Despite a large workforce of 586 million, India experiences jobless growth, with low workforce productivity levels and only 22% of the workforce engaged in the formal sector.
  3. India's workforce productivity is among the lowest globally, with GDP per capita at approximately $2,134 in 2023, significantly below the $12,000 threshold for upper-middle-income status.
  4. India's infrastructure investment as a percentage of GDP is around 5%, which is lower than the 7% to 8% needed to support rapid economic growth and development.
  5. Only about 3% of India's workforce has received formal vocational training, compared to over 50% in countries like Germany, indicating a significant gap in human capital development.
