Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 02, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its population ages. The country's projected GDP growth rates of around 6.5% to 7.3% are insufficient to meet the needed annual growth rates of 18.0% in nominal terms and 5.9% in PPP terms to reach the rich country threshold by 2035. Additionally, structural issues such as high youth unemployment, a brain drain, and a rapidly aging population in some regions further complicate India's path to achieving higher per capita incomes.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. Manufacturing contributes about 17% to India's GDP, which is significantly lower than the 25% to 30% seen in other rapidly industrializing economies like South Korea and China. This gap hinders India's ability to achieve rapid economic growth through industrialization.
  2. The formal sector in India accounts for only about 10% of total employment, with the informal sector dominating the labor market. This limits access to better job security and social benefits, impacting overall economic productivity.
  3. India's labor productivity growth rate is approximately 2.38% annually, which is lower than many other emerging economies. Higher productivity growth is crucial for sustained economic advancement.
  4. India's infrastructure investment as a percentage of GDP is around 5%, which is below the 7% to 8% recommended for developing countries by the World Bank. This shortfall affects economic efficiency and growth potential.
  5. Female labor force participation in India is about 32%, significantly lower than the male participation rate and below the global average. Increasing female participation could boost economic output by leveraging a larger share of the workforce.
