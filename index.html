Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 26, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak. The projected nominal GDP growth rate of around 7.0% is far below the required annual growth rate of 18.0% to reach the rich country threshold of $13,000 nominal per capita by 2035. Additionally, the current GNI per capita of $2,480.8 (nominal) and $7,340.0 (PPP) indicates a substantial gap to the rich country threshold, with only 10 years left before the demographic peak in 2035.

What Is Going Wrong

    1. Manufacturing Contribution Gap**
      Manufacturing contributes ~17% of GDP (2023 estimates), below the 25% target required for middle-income transition, as per NITI Aayog's Make in India 2.0 goals.
    1. Employment Elasticity Shortfall**
      Employment elasticity (jobs created per GDP growth unit) remains 0.2-0.3, requiring >0.5 to absorb 12 million annual labor entrants (RBI 2023 reports).
    1. Infrastructure Investment Deficit**
      Infrastructure spending at 4.5% of GDP (2023) trails the 6-8% of GDP threshold recommended by World Bank for emerging economies.
    1. Human Capital Development Lag**
      Mean years of schooling stagnate at 6.5 years (UNDP 2023), below the 9.1 years threshold for upper-middle-income nations.
    1. Total Factor Productivity Growth**
      TFP growth averages 1.1% annually (2015-2023, RBI data), less than half the 2.5%+ required for upper-middle-income transition.
