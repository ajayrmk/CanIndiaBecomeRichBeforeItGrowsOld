Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 17, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak. The projected actual growth rates of 7.0% in nominal terms and 5.0% in PPP terms fall short of the required annual growth rates of 18.0% and 5.9%, respectively, needed to reach the rich country threshold by 2035. Additionally, structural constraints such as low labor force participation (56.4%) and the need for substantial reforms to increase investment and productivity pose significant hurdles.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India's GDP growth rate is projected at 6.5% for the fiscal year ending March 2025, which is below the 8%+ growth rates often cited as necessary for rapid economic transformation. This gap highlights a shortfall in achieving high growth rates consistently.
  2. India needs to create about 90 million non-farm jobs by 2030 to absorb its workforce, but current job creation rates are significantly lower, with only about 7 million jobs created annually in recent years. This indicates a substantial shortfall in employment generation.
  3. India's labor productivity growth has been around 5% annually, which is lower than the 7-8% growth needed to match global benchmarks. This productivity gap hinders India's ability to compete effectively in the global market.
  4. India aims to spend about 10% of its GDP on infrastructure annually, but actual spending has often been below this target, averaging around 5-6%. This shortfall in infrastructure investment limits economic growth potential.
  5. India's education sector faces significant challenges, with only about 50% of the workforce having completed secondary education, compared to over 80% in many developed economies. This gap in human capital development hampers India's ability to transition to higher-value sectors.
