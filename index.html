Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 03, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak in 2035. The country needs an annual nominal growth rate of 18.0% and a PPP growth rate of 5.9% to reach the rich country threshold, but the projected actual growth rates are much lower, at 7.0% nominal and 5.0% PPP. Structural constraints include a sluggish manufacturing sector, persistent food inflation, and stagnant job growth, which hinder the implementation of comprehensive reforms necessary for sustained high growth. Additionally, India's current GNI per capita is $2,480.8, far below the rich country threshold of $13,000, requiring substantial and sustained economic acceleration.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India's GDP growth rate is projected at 6.5% for 2025, which is below the required 8% needed to achieve rapid economic development and compete with China's growth trajectory.
  2. India faces a significant job crisis with an unemployment rate of 4.2% and youth unemployment at 15%, despite needing to create millions of jobs annually to absorb its large workforce.
  3. India's workforce productivity is among the lowest globally, with the average size of land holdings being very small, contributing to low agricultural productivity.
  4. Only 46% of India's cultivable land is irrigated, highlighting a significant gap in agricultural infrastructure development.
  5. India's social welfare spending is relatively low at 8.6% of GDP, compared to OECD averages, which can impact human capital development.
