Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 06, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak in 2035. The projected nominal GDP growth rate of 7.0% falls short of the needed 18.0% annual increase to reach the rich country threshold of $13,000 nominal per capita by 2035. Additionally, structural constraints such as a large informal economy and the need for substantial reforms to boost productivity and investment rates hinder rapid economic acceleration.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India's GDP growth rate is projected at 6.5% to 6.8% for FY24/25, which is below the required sustained high growth rates of 8% to 10% needed to rapidly increase per capita income and become a high-income economy before aging.
  2. Despite a large workforce, India faces jobless growth, with low workforce productivity and a high unemployment rate among urban youth at 16.8% in FY24/25, indicating a need for more productive job creation.
  3. India's workforce productivity is among the lowest globally, with a significant gap in productivity compared to other major economies, which hampers economic growth and competitiveness.
  4. India's infrastructure spending is crucial for growth, but it faces challenges in execution and efficiency. For instance, government capex utilization was only 37.3% in the first half of FY24/25, lower than the previous year's 49%, indicating a need for better infrastructure development and utilization.
  5. India has high child malnutrition rates, with 35.5% of children under five years old being stunted, and a low female labor force participation rate, which are critical human capital gaps that need addressing to support sustainable economic growth.
