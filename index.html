Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of March 31, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before it grows old. The country's current GNI per capita is $2,480.8 (nominal) and $7,340.0 (PPP), far below the rich country threshold of $13,000 per capita in both nominal and PPP terms. To reach this threshold, India would need an annual nominal growth rate of 18.0% and a PPP growth rate of 5.9%, which are substantially higher than the projected actual growth rates of 7.0% (nominal) and 5.0% (PPP). Additionally, structural constraints such as high youth unemployment, a rapidly aging population, and a lack of comprehensive economic reforms hinder India's ability to achieve such high growth rates.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India's GDP growth rate is projected at 6.5% for 2025, which is below the required 8% needed to become a developed nation by 2047.
  2. The unemployment rate stands at 4.2%, with youth unemployment at 15%, indicating a significant gap in job creation for the 10 to 12 million young people entering the job market annually.
  3. Manufacturing contributes only 25% to India's workforce, compared to a desired higher share to boost productivity and exports.
  4. The government's capital expenditure is budgeted at 3.1% of GDP for FY 2025-26, which may not be sufficient to address the infrastructure gap needed for sustained growth.
  5. Education spending is currently at 4.6% of GDP, which needs to increase to around 6.5% by FY2048 to support India's young population and workforce requirements.

  3. Ashwini Vaishnaw (Minister of Railways, Electronics & IT, and Information & Broadcasting) - Vaishnaw's role in modernizing Indian Railways and integrating technology into governance has been pivotal. His efforts have strengthened the financials of the Railways, a key sector for economic growth. The introduction of new trains and station upgrades will enhance connectivity and efficiency, supporting economic development.

  4. Nitin Gadkari (Union Minister for Road Transport and Highways) - Gadkari's leadership in highway development has significantly expanded India's road network, enhancing connectivity and facilitating trade. Over 50,000 km of roads have been laid under his tenure, contributing to economic growth by improving infrastructure. His innovative approaches to contractual issues have reduced costs while maintaining quality.

  5. Sanjay Malhotra (Governor, Reserve Bank of India) - As the current RBI Governor, Malhotra plays a crucial role in monetary policy decisions, influencing interest rates and inflation control. His leadership is vital for maintaining economic stability and supporting growth through appropriate monetary measures. The RBI's policies under his guidance will impact India's financial sector and overall economic trajectory.
