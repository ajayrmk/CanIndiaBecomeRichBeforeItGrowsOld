Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 13, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become a rich country before its demographic peak in 2035. The projected actual growth rates of 7.0% (nominal) and 5.0% (PPP) fall short of the needed annual growth rates of 18.0% (nominal) and 5.9% (PPP) to reach the rich country threshold by 2035. Additionally, structural constraints such as low labor force participation and the need for accelerated reforms to boost investment and productivity hinder India's ability to sustain high growth over the next decade.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India's GDP growth rate is projected to be between 6.5% and 7.3% in the coming years, which is below the 8% to 10% required for rapid economic transformation to high-income status.
  2. Despite a large workforce, India faces jobless growth, with workforce productivity among the lowest globally. The unemployment rate remains high, especially among urban youth at 16.8% in FY24/25.
  3. India's workforce productivity is significantly lower than global standards, contributing to slower economic growth. The country ranks poorly in terms of productivity per worker compared to other major economies.
  4. India's infrastructure spending is around 5% of GDP, which is lower than the 8% to 10% needed to support rapid economic growth and development.
  5. India has a high child malnutrition rate, with 35.5% of children under five years old being stunted, which impacts future productivity and economic growth.
