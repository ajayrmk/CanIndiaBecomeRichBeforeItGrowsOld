Can India become rich before it grows old?

No

(as of April 01, 2025)

India faces significant challenges in achieving the required growth rates to become rich before its demographic peak. The projected nominal growth rate of 7.0% falls short of the needed 18.0% annual increase to reach the rich country threshold by 2035. Additionally, structural constraints such as food inflation, stagnant job growth, and trade deficits hinder long-term economic ambitions. Demographically, India has only about 10 years before its population peaks, which adds pressure to achieve rapid economic transformation.

What Is Going Wrong

  1. India's GDP growth rate is projected to be between 6.5% and 7.3% in the coming years, which is below the 8% to 10% required for rapid economic transformation to high-income status before demographic challenges intensify.
  2. Despite a large workforce, India faces jobless growth, with low workforce productivity levels and a high unemployment rate among urban youth at 16.8% in FY24/25, indicating a need for more effective job creation strategies.
  3. India's workforce productivity is among the lowest globally, with structural economic problems contributing to this issue. The country needs significant improvements in productivity to match global standards.
  4. India's infrastructure spending is crucial for growth, but it faces challenges in efficiently utilizing government capital expenditures. For instance, government capex utilization was only 37.3% in the first half of FY24/25, indicating a need for better infrastructure development and management.
  5. India's human capital challenges include high child malnutrition rates (35.5% of children under five are stunted) and low female labor force participation, which hampers economic growth and social development.

Key Individuals Responsible

  1. Nirmala Sitharaman (Finance Minister of India)
    As Finance Minister, Sitharaman plays a crucial role in shaping India's economic policies, including budgetary decisions and fiscal reforms. Her recent budgets have focused on tax cuts and infrastructure investments, aiming to boost economic growth. India's GDP growth is projected at 5% for 2025, reflecting her policy impacts. Her decisions directly influence the scale of impact across various sectors, affecting millions of people and businesses.

  2. Sanjay Malhotra (Governor, Reserve Bank of India)
    As the current RBI Governor, Malhotra holds significant authority over monetary policy, influencing interest rates and inflation control. His recent decisions, such as cutting interest rates, aim to stimulate economic growth amidst a slowdown. His role is critical in managing inflation and ensuring financial stability, which are key to India's economic trajectory.

  3. Parshottam Rupala (Minister of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying, and also involved in key economic committees)
    Although not directly a finance minister, Rupala's involvement in various economic committees and his role in sectors like agriculture can impact rural employment and income growth. His influence on agricultural policies can affect a large portion of India's workforce and contribute to overall economic stability.

  4. Ashwini Vaishnaw (Minister of Railways and Communications)
    Vaishnaw's dual role in railways and communications places him at the forefront of infrastructure development and digital transformation. His initiatives in these sectors can enhance connectivity, boost manufacturing, and increase service-led exports, contributing significantly to India's economic growth.

  5. Suman Bery (Vice Chairman, NITI Aayog)
    As Vice Chairman of NITI Aayog, Bery plays a pivotal role in formulating and implementing India's long-term development strategies. His influence extends to policy-making across sectors, including healthcare, education, and infrastructure, which are crucial for sustainable economic growth and addressing demographic challenges.
